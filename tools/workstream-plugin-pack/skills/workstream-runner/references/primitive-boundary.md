# Primitive Boundary

A workstream is one bounded execution unit around one objective. It is useful
because it gives a DRA a closed loop: frame, authority, evidence, execution,
review, repair, closure, and continuation.

Do not turn a normal workstream into:

- a program or portfolio;
- a parent container for subordinate workstreams;
- a recursive execution model;
- sequence authority over later workstreams;
- a live task board;
- architecture or product authority.

Phases, waves, lanes, agents, and review loops are internal mechanics inside
the active workstream. They help the DRA run the work; they do not create
child workstreams or another governance layer.

The DRA owns:

- synthesis and final judgment;
- scope and containment;
- authority order;
- claim strength and proof boundaries;
- finding disposition;
- repo and branch state;
- closure and handoff.

Agents, hooks, skills, templates, and records reduce mechanical process load.
They never replace DRA judgment.
