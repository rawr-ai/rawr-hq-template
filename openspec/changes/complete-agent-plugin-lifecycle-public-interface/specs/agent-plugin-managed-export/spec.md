## REMOVED Requirements

### Requirement: Explicit artifact-backed export application
**Reason**: Destination export is outside the corrected curated lifecycle outcome and its publication substrate belongs to the dedicated full architecture migration.
**Migration**: Retire the lifecycle command, service module, bindings, ports, and controller composition without a stub, alias, fallback, or replacement implementation.

### Requirement: One truthful ledger per canonical destination
**Reason**: The curated lifecycle controller no longer owns destination state.
**Migration**: Transfer the useful ledger requirement to the dedicated full architecture migration.

### Requirement: Scope-bounded planning and retirement
**Reason**: Export planning and retirement move to the destination architecture, outside curated lifecycle ownership.
**Migration**: Preserve packaging and provider convergence; do not retain an unreachable export planner as compatibility code.

### Requirement: Explicit collision and native-home exclusion
**Reason**: The attempted cross-owner exclusion required a publication primitive disproportionate to the corrected outcome.
**Migration**: Remove the provider-home aggregate and export collision bridge. Provider lifecycle remains explicit and point-addressed; destination ownership belongs to the dedicated migration.

### Requirement: Payload publication is atomic and path-safe
**Reason**: C6 must not manufacture a shared native primitive or generalized publication framework for a capability being retired.
**Migration**: Transfer useful publication requirements to the dedicated full architecture migration.

### Requirement: Verified export transaction and truthful inverse actions
**Reason**: Export transaction and inverse-action authority leave the lifecycle controller together.
**Migration**: Retire their service and capsule surfaces rather than preserving a second state owner.

### Requirement: Repeated converged export is read-only
**Reason**: No lifecycle export operation remains reachable.
**Migration**: The dedicated destination architecture must define its own convergence law when implemented.

### Requirement: Export deletion is ledger-bounded and path-safe
**Reason**: The curated lifecycle controller no longer owns destination deletion.
**Migration**: Transfer the useful deletion boundary to the dedicated full architecture migration; add no replacement here.

### Requirement: Export cannot mutate adjacent authorities
**Reason**: Structural retirement is stronger than retaining a dormant cross-authority export implementation.
**Migration**: Provider, Oclif, packaging, content, and channel authorities remain disjoint; destination work moves to its dedicated owner.

### Requirement: Export results are exhaustive state transitions
**Reason**: The export procedure and its public result schema are removed from the oRPC service surface.
**Migration**: A future destination service must define its own TypeBox boundary and transition model rather than importing this lifecycle result.
