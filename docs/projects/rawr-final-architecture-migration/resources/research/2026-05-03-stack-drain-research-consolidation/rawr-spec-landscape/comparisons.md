# Cross-locus comparisons — rawr-spec-landscape

Five depth investigations committed positions on the RAWR spec corpus. Their positions interlock — three converge from independent paths, two stand in dialectical tension that the draft must engage. Every cross-locus dynamic below is a load-bearing argumentative beat the final report must surface, not gesture at.

## Tension 1: FOUR-tier structural verdict vs. THREE-tier steelman (L1 stands alone in dialectic)

- **L1 — platform-level-shape** ([[locus-interim-platform-level-shape]]) commits: RAWR decomposes into FOUR tiers — Core Runtime / Semantic-Composition / Coordination / Governance — because the Authoring Classifier and Factory Bundle Export constitute a distinct semantic engineering tier whose membership is incoherent inside Coordination or Governance. Confidence ~70%.
- **The corpus's own steelman:** the Habitat SDK Layers spec §1 collapses to three lines ("Habitat SDK = nervous system; Coordination = ongoing work; Governance = authority and adaptation") and the System Architecture spec §4.11 uses a three-line authority law (shell drives what / stewards drive how / governance decides whether). A defensible reading collapses Semantic-Composition into either an "SDK-adjacent build-time tooling" sub-region of Core Runtime or into "pre-runtime governance" inside Governance.
- **The cross-locus dynamic:** **Dialectical, internal to L1.** This is the only tension where one locus must defend its committed position against a credible counter-position drawn from the same corpus's META specs. The four-tier reading is not contradicted by any other locus — but it is also not redundantly endorsed. L4 grades cells per-spec without naming tiers; L3 and L5 surface gaps without depending on tier count; L2 names vendors without depending on tier count. So L1 is the lone tier-decision authority, and the steelman three-tier reading must be defeated visibly, not silently.
- **How the draft should engage this:** §3 (Committed Platform-Level Decomposition) must lead with the user's three-tier proposal, name the steelman three-tier reading drawn from the META specs' own §1 summaries, then defend the four-tier choice using L1's load-bearing argument: the team-topology test ("classifier engineers, bundle-packaging engineers, RFD authors, and steward-authority designers do not share a primary engineering concern"). The defense must explicitly state which Habitat SDK Layers L# layer maps to which tier so the four-tier and nine-layer models reconcile.
- **Calibration:** L1 explicitly names the mind-changer — "if a revised Habitat SDK Layers spec explicitly mapped both the Authoring Classifier and Factory Bundle Export into L6 (Coordination) or L8 (Governance) rather than into L2/L9, that would constitute direct evidence." The corpus does NOT contain such evidence. Absent that evidence, the four-tier reading wins on team-topology and forbidden-pattern grounds.

## Tension 2: Per-spec A grades vs. system-level B+ verdict (L4 vs. L1)

- **L1 — platform-level-shape** ([[locus-interim-platform-level-shape]]) commits a structural verdict: the four-tier landscape decomposition holds. It does not commit a system-level grade.
- **L4 — authoritative-vs-shape-correct-reconciliation** ([[locus-interim-authoritative-vs-shape-correct-reconciliation]]) commits a completeness verdict: per-spec grades are A/A−/B+ but the system-level grade is B+ (not A) on integration-law grounds, because three blocking seams cross spec boundaries unrealized — (1) Inngest connect worker as a bootgraph-integrated Effect Layer; (2) agent-role direct WorkflowDispatcher emission rights; (3) auth-specific compiler gate closure. Confidence high.
- **The cross-locus dynamic:** **Apparent tension, resolved on closer reading.** A naive reader hits "thirteen specs at A or A−" (per L1's individual spec mentions plus the structural cleanliness) and then reads "system-level B+" (L4) and concludes a contradiction. The two verdicts are NOT on the same axis. L1 grades structural decomposition coherence; L4 grades cross-spec seam closure. Both can be true. The draft's methodology (§2) must make this two-axis reading visible: per-spec grades describe inside-this-spec completeness; system-level grades describe seam-closure-across-specs completeness; a corpus can be A on the first and B+ on the second simultaneously.
- **How the draft should engage this:** §2 must define the rubric explicitly with this two-axis distinction. §9's heatmap can grade two ways: per-spec coverage (mostly A) and per-seam closure (B+ with three blocking gaps). Without this distinction, readers will perceive contradiction where there is decomposition.
- **Calibration:** L4 high confidence on the three blocking seams; L1 medium-high on the structural take. The combined claim — "four tiers, structurally clean, system-level B+ on integration-law" — is robust because the two verdicts are about different things.

## Tension 3: Universal-template vs. universal-principle (L2 stands alone with built-in steelman)

- **L2 — vendor-integration-pattern-universality** ([[locus-interim-vendor-integration-pattern-universality]]) commits: the standing-on-shoulders pattern is a universal *principle* (10-rule descriptor-first checklist) instantiated in irreducibly bespoke per-vendor *shapes*. "Universal template" is too strong, "principles only" is too weak. Top exemplars: Effect, Inngest. Falls short: Drizzle, Authoring Classifier LLM. Confidence >85%.
- **The cross-locus dynamic:** **Internal dialectic with high-confidence resolution.** A skilled critic could push toward a universal-template reading: "every vendor integration uses Resource + Provider + descriptor lowering; that's a template." L2's own evidence shows that's wrong — oRPC's seam (`EffectExecutionDescriptor` capture + error bridge) is structurally different from Inngest's (`WorkflowDispatcherDescriptor` + `FunctionBundle` lowering chain) is structurally different from Auth's (`AuthVerifierResource` + provider families + `VerifiedPrincipal`) is structurally different from Deployment's (`TargetProfile` compilation chain). The principle is a universal; the shapes are bespoke because each vendor's zone of strength is structurally different.
- **How the draft should engage this:** §7 must lead with the 10-rule checklist as the universal principle, then walk per-vendor seam topology to demonstrate the bespoke-shapes claim. The Inngest case is the diagnostic: it earns Grade A on "used at strength" (clean delegation: RAWR owns cold definitions + lowering chain; Inngest owns durability/retry/replay) but Grade B on "integration-cashed" (event-key/signing rotation, idempotency conventions, dead-letter, schema evolution all unspecified). Two-axis grading per vendor is the visible argumentative beat that defeats the universal-template reading.
- **Calibration:** L2 explicitly names the mind-changer — "evidence that a single structural template (e.g., a `defineVendorIntegration({ resource, facade, forbiddenSet, descriptor, gate })` macro) exists or is specified that all vendor integrations actually use without per-vendor seam customization." No such macro is in the corpus.

## Tension 4: Convergent gap surfacing (L3 + L5 reinforce; L4 partially overlaps)

- **L3 — dont-own-still-manage-frontier-coverage** ([[locus-interim-dont-own-still-manage-frontier-coverage]]) commits three flagged gaps as "addressed only at the policy layer, not at the integration-law layer": Inngest event interface, classifier LLM call, bundle registry/signing. Confidence >80%.
- **L5 — operational-substrate-gaps** ([[locus-interim-operational-substrate-gaps]]) commits 14 real gaps + 8 bookmarked deferrals; top 5 P0/P1 gaps include bundle signing/attestation, OTel/HyperDX propagation, MFA/step-up, idempotency-key + dead-letter, MAWE profile→manifest. Confidence high.
- **L4 — authoritative-vs-shape-correct-reconciliation** commits three blocking integration-law seams: Inngest connect-worker Effect Layer, agent-role WorkflowDispatcher emission rights, auth-specific compiler gates.
- **The cross-locus dynamic:** **Convergence from three independent investigations.** Both L3 and L5 independently surface bundle signing, the Inngest event interface, the classifier LLM call. L4 separately surfaces the Inngest connect-worker as a different facet of "Inngest is incompletely integrated." Three independent investigators converging on the same gap pattern is the highest-confidence signal in the corpus.
- **How the draft should engage this:** §8 (Don't-Own-Still-Manage Frontier) and §10 (Open Gaps) must both name these as the canonical recommendation set. The structure: (a) classify each gap as either *integration-law debt* (L4-style: a runtime claim asserted by a shape-correct spec but not locked in the authoritative spec) or *operational-substrate gap* (L5-style: a field declared without an enforcement protocol); (b) name the next spec / next section that closes it; (c) for each gap, note which loci surfaced it. Inngest in particular needs a unified treatment: the connect-worker Effect Layer (L4), the event interface disciplines (L3 + L5), and the cross-process trace propagation (L5) are facets of one vendor under-cashed at three boundary classes (mount, emit, observe).
- **Calibration:** Convergence dramatically raises confidence. L4's three blocking seams + L5's 14 operational gaps + L3's three flagged gaps consolidate into approximately 8-10 distinct top-priority gaps with overlap. The recommendation list is robust. **Post-corpus-critic adjustment:** L4's `agent-role direct-emission rights` blocking seam carries MEDIUM-HIGH confidence (was HIGH) — the runtime-realization spec §19 WorkflowDispatcher subsection was not read at maximum depth and may already partially enumerate per-role emission rights. The other two blocking seams (Inngest connect-worker Effect Layer; auth-specific compiler gates) remain HIGH confidence. The §10 gaps section should mark the agent-role-emission seam as 'medium-high confidence gap.'

## Tension 5: Shape-correct discipline vs. reservation discipline (L3 + L4 + L5 calibrate the rubric)

- **L3** classifies silences as either "conscious deferral" (named seam + named owner + trigger) or "coverage gap" (no seam, no owner, no integration law). L3's exemplars of conscious deferral: token library selection (Auth), specific IdP (Auth), Railway/Railpack API automation (Deployment), blue/green/canary rollout (Deployment).
- **L4** classifies runtime-shape claims as either (a) already in runtime spec, (b) implied but not realized, (c) contradicted, (d) deferred work named in runtime spec. L4's exemplars: ServiceBindingCacheKey is (a); OpenShell direct-emission is (b); durable-event-bus ownership is (d).
- **L5** classifies operational silences into 8 bookmarked deferrals and 14 real gaps using essentially the same rubric.
- **The cross-locus dynamic:** **Convergent rubric across three loci.** L3, L4, and L5 use compatible classification schemes — name-the-owner-or-it's-a-gap. The convergence is itself a methodology finding: RAWR's spec discipline is mature enough that "a real gap" is structurally distinguishable from "a deliberate deferral with a named handoff," and the platform's quality is in part measured by the ratio. Three independent investigators arrived at the same rubric without coordination.
- **How the draft should engage this:** §2 (Methodology) should define the deferral-vs-gap rubric explicitly using L3's three-part test: named seam, named owner, named trigger condition. §4-§6 per-tier sections, §7 vendor table, §8 frontier section, and §10 gaps list all use this rubric consistently. Without a unified rubric the heatmap (§9) becomes a list of opinions; with the rubric, it is auditable.
- **Calibration:** All three loci are high or near-high confidence. The rubric is the shared methodological substrate.

## Cross-cutting calibration note

Three of five tensions (Tensions 3, 4, 5) are **convergences across loci** — independent investigators reinforcing the same finding. These are the highest-confidence claims the draft should make. One tension (Tension 2) is a **scope-disambiguation** that requires explicit two-axis methodology, not a real disagreement. One tension (Tension 1) is a **standalone dialectical** beat where L1's structural verdict must defeat the steelman three-tier reading on team-topology and forbidden-pattern grounds.

The single most argumentatively load-bearing beat is **Tension 1** — the four-tier vs. three-tier defense in §3. If the draft handles Tension 1 weakly, the Semantic-Composition tier collapses and the per-tier sections (§4-§6) lose their structural anchor. If the draft handles Tension 1 strongly, the rest of the structure pays for itself.

The single most consequential cross-cutting finding is **Inngest under three boundary classes** (mount = L4, emit = L3+L5, observe = L5). The draft should treat Inngest in §7 with explicit two-axis grading (used-at-strength A; integration-cashed B), then name the three boundary-class gaps in §8 and §10 as the canonical worked example of "policy-layer addressed, integration-law incomplete."
